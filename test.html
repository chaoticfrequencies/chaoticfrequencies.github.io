PAUSE. LOOK. NOTICE.

In our fast everyday city life, we often forget to notice. Hardly do ever take notice of the shapes that clouds make, or how the chipped paint on the wall looks like a particular animal. We could only do that in childhood, when our understanding was limited and when we used our imagination to fill in the gaps. What we start losing as we grow up is probably awareness of the moments–in being so caught up with the nostalgia of the past, or the worries about the future, the beauty of the present slips by us. And, then we can only look back upon it, reminisce and tell stories. Or, we spend hours in bed at night anticipating the next day, another such moment. The series of photographs that I would like to present would be a window to my perception of the world, my attempt to capture the perfection in the world around us just as it is. In the process, there is also the hope to find and present a new way of seeing. Perfection might indeed be a relative concept, but these photographs will urge you to seek perfection in the mundane.
